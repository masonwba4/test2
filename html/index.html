Oasis: A Brief History

Formation and Early Years

Oasis was formed in Manchester, England, in 1991 by brothers Liam and Noel Gallagher. The band started by performing in small venues and gained attention with their energetic performances and catchy songwriting. Their style drew heavily on classic rock influences, including The Beatles and The Rolling Stones.

Rise to Fame

The release of their debut album, Definitely Maybe in 1994, quickly established Oasis as one of the leading bands of the Britpop movement. The follow-up, (What's the Story) Morning Glory? in 1995, became a worldwide success, featuring hits like "Wonderwall" and "Don't Look Back in Anger."

Key Events Timeline

  • 1991: Oasis forms in Manchester.
  • 1994: Release of Definitely Maybe.
  • 1995: Release of (What's the Story) Morning Glory?.
  • 2000: Release of Standing on the Shoulder of Giants.
  • 2009: Band announces split after Noel Gallagher leaves.

Legacy

Oasis is widely regarded as one of the most influential British rock bands of the 1990s. Their anthemic songs, outspoken personalities, and Britpop rivalry with bands like Blur left a lasting mark on music culture. Even after their breakup, both Noel and Liam continue to produce music in their respective solo projects.
